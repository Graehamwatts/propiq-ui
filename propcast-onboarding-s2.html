1. Welcome. My name is [first name last name], and I'm a licensed real estate agent helping people in [your primary city] and the surrounding areas buy and sell homes. I've been doing this for several years, and I love what I do.

2. Here's what I'm seeing in the market this week. Inventory is up about twelve percent compared to this time last year. Median days on market is hovering around twenty-eight. Most of the activity is happening in the eight-hundred-thousand to one-point-two-million range.

3. If you're a first-time buyer, this is a window worth paying attention to. Sellers are more willing to negotiate than they were six months ago. You won't have five competing offers on every home. That doesn't mean wait forever — but it does mean you have room to breathe.

4. For sellers — here's the honest truth. The listings that sell fastest are priced right from day one, presented well, and marketed everywhere buyers actually look. If your house has been sitting for thirty days with no showings, something is off, and it's almost always one of those three things.

5. Let me walk you through a neighborhood I know well. Picture tree-lined streets, restored craftsman homes, a coffee shop on the corner that always has a line. Schools rank in the top ten percent. Average home value just over one-point-four million. That's the kind of block that holds value even when the broader market wobbles.

6. Numbers matter, so let's say them out loud. Two hundred thirty thousand. Five hundred forty-seven thousand. One-point-two million. Three-point-eight percent. Seven percent. Twenty-one days. One hundred twelve showings. Four offers. Closed.

7. I know buying or selling a home can feel overwhelming. There's a lot of money on the table, a lot of emotions, and a lot of decisions that feel permanent. My job is to take the weight off — to give you honest advice, real data, and the patience to move at your pace.

8. One thing I wish more buyers knew — your monthly payment is not just the mortgage. Factor in property taxes, homeowner's insurance, HOA dues if applicable, and a realistic maintenance budget. Usually that's another fifteen to twenty percent on top of the mortgage itself. Knowing that up front saves painful surprises later.

9. Alright, so — quick check-in. How are you feeling about your situation? Ready to move? Just looking? Somewhere in the middle? All three are totally valid. The best thing you can do right now is get real numbers on your own market, even if you don't act on them for six months.

10. If this was helpful, hit follow so you don't miss the next one. If you want me to run the numbers on your specific situation, my contact info is right there. No pressure, no spam, just real answers. Thanks for listening — I'll see you next time.

Tip: Don't read like a robot. Imagine you're on a call with a new client — some of this is explanation, some is encouragement, some is gentle challenge. That variety is what makes the clone sound like you.
